Research Interests
  • I am in general interested in Algorithm Design/Analysis while my focus is on Game Theory (in particular, application-driven Social Choice Theory).
  • By and large, I am working on various 'Scheduling' problems. Here is a brief description.
      •     My research concerns modeling group scheduling problems in which a group of human agents attempt to agree on a schedule which can be a corporate meeting or a social event. In general, we can imagine that an event organizer requests invitees to reveal their availability, and then the organizer simply chooses the "best" time to schedule an event according to some measure of optimality.
          There are several aspects to be considered: truthfulness of agents (in revealing their availability), promptness of agents (in terms of how long it takes for agents to respond to the organizer), quality of the resulting schedule (in terms of how satisfactory it is for the attending agents), efficiency of the scheduling process (in terms of how much inconvenience was caused), and more.
          In a setting where we assume that all agents are truthful and prompt, we can formulate the group scheduling problem as an algorithmic optimization problem in the sense that our goal becomes to design an efficient scheduling procedure (according to some measure of efficiency).
          In a more realistic setting where agents may lie about their availability, our goal is then to prevent agents from having incentives to lie while the scheduling procedure can still yield a "good" outcome (with some measure of "good").
  • While I only described scheduling problems in social settings, I am also interested in expanding my research to other kinds of scheduling problems (such as job scheduling), which is more related to systems design.
